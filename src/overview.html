Since 2000, floods have grown more frequent and severe, costing the United States more than $800 billion in damage to homes, businesses, and critical infrastructure—according to the National Centers for Environmental Information (part of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration). The expense of adapting to more frequent and severe storms is projected to increase over the next several decades, placing a premium on the need to take action now to reduce flood impacts in the future.

Noting the recent increase in such efforts, The Pew Charitable Trusts—in partnership with Dewberry—examined policies across the country and identified 13 states or cities that have adopted measures resulting in effective flood mitigation activities. Detailed in this set of briefs, the policies fall into three categories: 1) using existing funds for mitigation by redirecting revenue and spending, 2) creating revenue sources for mitigation, and 3) creating smarter regulations to reduce flood risk.
