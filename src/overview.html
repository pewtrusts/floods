Devastating floods are on the rise. Since 2000, floods have grown more frequent and severe, costing the United States more than $800 billion in damage to homes, businesses, and critical infrastructure. The expense of preparing for more severe storms is similarly projected to multiply over the next several decades, placing a premium on the need to take swift action to reduce the impacts of future floods.

Research shows that every $1 spent on risk reduction measures saves an average of $6 on post-disaster response and recovery costs. Efforts to take advantage of this return on investment are beginning to take hold at all levels of government.
